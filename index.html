Garbage Treatment Power Plant

The New Garbage Treatment Plant presents an innovative, eco-friendly solution for waste management. By combining steam power, solar energy, and bio-fertilizer production, the project effectively tackles electricity shortages and environmental concerns. Its emphasis on recycling, zero waste, and sustainable practices highlights its potential to improve urban waste management systems.

Electricity Generation: Dry and wet garbage heat a boiler to create steam, which powers a turbine. Solid garbage is heated to produce light, which reflects on solar panels to generate additional electricity.

Fertilizer Production: A mixture of cow dung, wet garbage, and gases from factories is processed to create nutrient-rich fertilizer.

Recycling: Scrap materials are melted and reshaped for reuse in factories.

The project emphasizes zero waste, sustainable energy production, and eco-friendly practices, reducing pollution and maximizing resource recovery.

Self Navigating Vehicle

This project involves designing an autonomous vehicle using an Arduino microcontroller that can navigate its environment efficiently by avoiding obstacles and following a predefined path. It is equipped with ultrasonic sensors for real-time obstacle detection and IR sensors for line following. The L298N motor driver controls the vehicle's movement based on sensor inputs. The system operates in dual modes—line-following mode for guided movement and obstacle avoidance mode for free navigation. By integrating smart sensors and decision-making algorithms, this project enables efficient, hands-free navigation, making it ideal for applications like automated transportation and robotics.

Automatic Plant Irrigation System

This project aims to design an automatic irrigation system using the IC 555 timer as a moisture sensor and control unit. The system monitors soil moisture levels and activates a water pump when the soil is dry, ensuring optimal watering for plants. The IC 555 is configured as a comparator, detecting changes in resistance from soil moisture sensors. When moisture drops below a set threshold, the 555 timer triggers a relay to turn on the water pump. Once the soil is adequately moist, the pump automatically turns off. This system helps conserve water, reduce manual effort, and maintain plant health efficiently.
